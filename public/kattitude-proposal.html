01 · The Opportunity

You create the art. The platform runs the studio.

Kattitude has an established presence — kattitude.com, a Shopify store, a loyal client base, and a team of working artists. What comes next is a central operations hub: a single system that consolidates appointment tracking, deposit management, artist schedules, client records, and daily studio administration. Today those pieces live across DMs, memory, and paper. This platform brings them together into one dashboard, accessible by Kat and her artists from any device.

Phase 1 is the studio operations platform. The client-facing landing page is the front door — built around the Kattitude brand and connected directly to the booking and client management flow behind it.

I built this platform for my own business and it's been running in production. Your studio gets a custom version of that same system, shaped around how you operate.

What changes for you
  • Operations move into the system — Appointment tracking, deposit management, artist scheduling, and client records all live in the dashboard. What lives in memory or on paper today becomes searchable, trackable, and always current.
  • Artists have their own access — Each artist logs in to see their schedule, review client reference photos before a session, and check upcoming appointments. Scheduling visibility replaces the back-and-forth.
  • Everything visible at a glance — Revenue, outstanding deposits, booked appointments, client history — one screen, any device. Information that used to require digging is always in front of you.

03 · How It Fits Into Daily Operations

The dashboard in studio life.

The dashboard becomes the operational backbone of the studio — the place Kat and her artists start and end every day.

Starting the Day
  • The dashboard opens to today's appointments, which artists are checked in, and any deposits outstanding for upcoming sessions.
  • Clients with appointments that day have their reference photos, session notes, and signed waivers accessible from the same screen — reviewed before they walk in the door.
  • Booking requests from the website appear in the queue, ready to review, approve, schedule, and confirm a deposit.
During the Day
  • Artists clock in from their phone or a studio tablet. Their active session is logged. Breaks are tracked.
  • When a client checks in, the artist pulls up reference photos and session notes directly from the booking record.
  • Payments and merch purchases process through the same system — Stripe on any device.
End of Day
  • Artists clock out. Hours are logged and available for scheduling reference.
  • Completed sessions are marked. Client records update — their history is already there when they book next time.
  • Revenue, sessions completed, and outstanding deposits reflect on the dashboard overview.
What's Next — Phase 2
Once Phase 1 is running and the studio's operations are in the system, Phase 2 adds an AI-powered booking engine. Clients upload reference photos during booking and receive an estimate based on complexity, placement, size, and style — calibrated against your artists' actual session history. Smart scheduling shows real availability based on each artist's speed and current workload. Phase 2 carries its own scope, timeline, and proposal, and launches once Phase 1 is live and your data is flowing.
